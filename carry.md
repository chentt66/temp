### 1. Term Structure Arbitrage ("Curve" Optimization)

Currently, you are locked into a **3-Month** tenor. But the "Basis" (the profit) is not flat across time. Sometimes the 1-month basis is huge (annualized), while the 3-month is tight.

* **The Research:** Compare the annualized hedged yield of **1M vs. 3M vs. 6M** tenors for the same currency.
* **The Strategy:** Instead of blindly rolling into the 3M bill, the algorithm dynamically selects the **"Steepest" point on the Basis Curve**.
* *Example:* If 1M annualized basis is 50bps and 3M is 20bps, invest in the 1M bill three times (rolling monthly) instead of one 3M bill.


* **Resume Bullet:** *"Conducted term-structure analysis on the Cross-Currency Basis curve, identifying optimal hedging tenors (1M vs. 3M) to maximize annualized roll-down yield."*

### 2. Statistical Entry/Exit Signals (Mean Reversion)

### 3. Transaction Cost Analysis (The "Net" Reality)

True arbitrage disappears when you account for the Bid-Ask spread. A theoretical 10bps profit might actually be a -5bps loss if liquidity is poor.

* **The Research:** Overlay **Bid-Ask spread data** on your FX Forwards and T-Bills.
* **The Strategy:** Calculate the **"Break-Even Basis."** Determine the minimum spread required to overcome transaction costs.
* **Resume Bullet:** *"Integrated Bid-Ask spread dynamics into the backtest engine to calculate 'Net Realizable Basis', filtering out ~20% of trades where transaction friction eroded the theoretical arbitrage profit."*
